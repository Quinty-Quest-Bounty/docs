## Why Pull-Based?

Quinty uses a **pull-based withdrawal** pattern instead of pushing funds directly to recipients. This is a security best practice that:

- Prevents reentrancy attacks
- Avoids failed transactions blocking other payments
- Lets users claim funds at their own pace

## How It Works

When you earn funds (winning a bounty, completing a quest, getting a deposit refund), the amount is **credited to your balance** inside the contract. It is not sent to your wallet automatically.

To receive your funds, you must call the withdraw function.

## Checking Your Balance

You can check your pending balance at any time:

```solidity
// For ETH
pendingBalance(address(0), yourAddress)

// For ERC-20 (e.g., USDC)
pendingBalance(usdcAddress, yourAddress)
```

In the frontend, your pending balance is shown on your profile page.

## Withdrawing

### ETH

Call `withdrawETH()` on either the Quinty or Quest contract. Your entire pending ETH balance is transferred to your wallet.

### ERC-20 (USDC)

Call `withdrawToken(tokenAddress)` with the token's contract address. Your entire pending balance for that token is transferred.

<Note>
  Withdrawals are always available, even when the contract is paused. This ensures users can always access their funds.
</Note>

## When Funds Are Credited

| Event | Who Gets Credited | Amount |
|-------|------------------|--------|
| Creator selects winners | Winners | Prize amount + deposit |
| Creator selects winners | Non-winners | Deposit refund |
| Creator selects fewer winners than slots | Creator | Unused prize tiers |
| Slash triggered | All submitters | Slash share + deposit |
| Slash triggered | Creator | Remaining after slash |
| Quest entry approved | Solver | Reward per qualifier |
| Quest finalized | Creator | Unused escrow |
| Quest cancelled | Creator | Full escrow |
| No submissions + refund | Creator | Full escrow |
