description: "How to create and fund a bounty"
---

## Before You Start

- Connect your wallet via Privy (email, Google, or existing wallet)
- Have enough ETH or USDC on Base Sepolia for the prizes + gas

## Step 1: Create the Bounty

Navigate to **Bounties** and click **Create Bounty**. Fill in:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Title | Short description of the task |
| Description | Full requirements and deliverables |
| Prize tiers | Amount for each rank (1st, 2nd, 3rd, etc.) |
| Open deadline | When submissions close |
| Judging deadline | When you must select winners by |
| Slash percentage | Penalty if you miss the judging deadline (25-50%) |
| Token | ETH or USDC |

## Step 2: Fund the Escrow

When you confirm the transaction, the total prize amount is transferred to the smart contract. For ERC-20 tokens (USDC), you'll need to approve the token transfer first.

The funds are locked until you select winners or the slash mechanism triggers.

## Step 3: Wait for Submissions

Submitters pay a 1% deposit when they submit work. Deposits are refunded to all submitters (winners and non-winners) when the bounty resolves.

## Step 4: Select Winners

After the open deadline passes, review submissions and select winners:

1. Go to your bounty page
2. Review each submission's IPFS proof
3. Rank the winners (first place gets `prizes[0]`, second gets `prizes[1]`, etc.)
4. Confirm the transaction

All payouts are credited to each winner's pull balance. They can withdraw whenever they want.

## Tips

- **Set realistic deadlines.** Give submitters enough time to do quality work, and give yourself enough time to judge.
- **Slash percentage matters.** A higher slash percentage (40-50%) incentivizes you to judge on time and gives submitters more confidence.
- **Fewer winners than slots is fine.** If you set 3 prize tiers but only 2 submissions are good, you can select 2 winners. The unused prize is refunded.

## What Happens If You Don't Judge?

If you miss the judging deadline, anyone can call `triggerSlash()`. The slash percentage of your escrowed funds is split equally among submitters, and the rest is returned to you.
